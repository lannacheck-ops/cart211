Case Notes — Corkboard Connections

I finally pinned all the threads—OmniCore at the center.
Phone companies, shopping platforms, food delivery apps,
and even photo-editing services. A perfect spiderweb. And we’re the flies.

It’s almost funny. In their TACs, OmniCore swears,
“Your privacy remains with us.”
Yet here it is—mapped out in front of me—our so-called “privacy”
being handed off to every company willing to pay.
If this is what “remaining with us” looks like,
I’d hate to see what leaving us looks like.

And the pattern matches everything I found in the Data Exhaust report.
The phone company knows how long I talk.
The restaurant apps know where I eat.
The shopping platforms know my motives—holiday gifts, stress buys, celebrations.
The photo-editing services know where I’ve been,
down to the coordinates stamped in every image.

OmniCore collects the traces.
These companies refine them.
Together, they know every move we make before we even make it.

And they call it business.
“Partnerships.”
“Service improvements.”
But when every pin on my corkboard is a place where my data is
sold, traded, or repurposed for profit.
There’s only one word that fits: exploitation.
