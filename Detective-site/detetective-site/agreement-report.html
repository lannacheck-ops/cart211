Detective’s Notes – Terms & Conditions Review

Service Enhancement Metrics.
That phrase keeps circling my mind.
What exactly are they enhancing… and for whose benefit?
Companies don’t choose words by accident. “Enhancement” sounds helpful, almost generous. But metrics?
Metrics are measurements.
Measurements of me.
I need to dig deeper.

Behavioral Residues.
This one is even stranger.
Residue implies something left behind—traces, fragments.
So what traces am I leaving that they’re collecting?
Clicks? Pauses? Hesitations?
Why does a company need the “residue” of my behaviour?
Feels more like surveillance than service.

Data Exhaust.
Exhaust… like fumes from a machine.
Leftover emissions.
Except I’m the machine here.
Everything I do—scrolling, typing, browsing—burns fuel they claim has no value.
If it’s worthless exhaust, why store it?
Why build a whole system around it?
Something isn’t adding up.

Remains With Us.
Us.
That word’s too vague to be harmless.
Who exactly is included in “us”?
Employees? Third-party apps? “Trusted partners”?
Advertisers? Data brokers?
If they won’t name them, they’re hiding something.
Whatever “remains with us” probably doesn’t stay there for long.
