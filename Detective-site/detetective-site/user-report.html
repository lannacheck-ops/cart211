Case Notes — User Experience

User Experience. “We improve your experience.” “We show you what you want.”
Does improvement count as a swarm of ads at every coner of my screen?

These suspiciously tailored suggestions coming in as soon as I got closer to uncovering this case.
Almost like they were trying to distract me… or stop me.

The strange part? These ads looked familiar. Too familiar.
The restaurant I visited last week.
Holiday gift suggestions I only hinted at in my search bar.
Even a reminder about that long phone call I made.
All turned into some promo I didn't ask for.

The same things I found on the Data Exhaust page.
So that’s what they meant by “enhancing my experience.”
Taking the most personal scraps of my life...
The GPS tags, the shopping motives, the call lengths—and turning them into bait.

They sugarcoat it well by saying “We’re personalizing things for you.”
But if this is help, why does it feel like obstruction?
Why is every ad placed exactly where it blocks my path forward?
Almost as if they’re weaponizing my own data against me—
using it to keep me from uncovering what they don’t want me to see.
