Case Notes — Data Exhaust

Data Exhaust. That term keeps bothering me.
Sounds harmless enough—like smoke from an engine, something that fades into thin air and disappears.
But the more I dig, the more I realize: this “exhaust” isn’t drifting anywhere.
It's being collected.

They say data exhaust has “no value.”

The GPS location of every picture I take… that’s not exhaust.
That’s a trail of everywhere I’ve been.

Even the length of my phone calls—why keep that?
What could someone learn from how long I talk, who I talk to, or how often?

And the restaurants I eat at… the places I spend time.
They call it exhaust, but these details trace patterns—habits, routines, weak spots.
If someone knows where I eat, they know where to find me.

Then there’s the shopping. Not just what I buy, but why.
Holiday gifts, birthday surprises, stress purchases.
It’s not just fumes. It’s my fingerprints, left behind everywhere I go.

Whatever this system is, it’s not collecting exhaust.
It’s collecting me.
